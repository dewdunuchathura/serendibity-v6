Culture, Safari & Wildlife

Home›Tours›Culture, Safari & Wildlife

Buy our classic Yala safari & get a 50% discounted night at a luxury campsite.

Ruhunu Wellassa area is one of the most popular tourist attractions in the country, mainly due to Yala National Park — a leopard kingdom of the world.

This is a historically important area in the country. The area is still not affected by the modern city and rapid development activities. Agricultural lands, farmers and rural village life continue peacefully. The simple life style of the villagers bounded with nature is fascinating. Vast rice paddy fields, forested villages, mountains, ancient man-made lakes, numerous Buddhist archaeological sites make a perfect vacation in Sri Lanka.

According to ancient native literature and archaeological studies, this area has been inhabited by Homo sapiens more than 120,000 years ago. This area also has numerous ruins of ancient Buddhist architecture. This was a densely populated area before the British invasion of the country. Subsequently, the area was abandoned after the Wellassa massacre in 1818.

This is an area with ample tourist attractions including wildlife, local culture, arts and crafts, village lifestyle, rice farming and fishing.

The secret life of nocturnal animals: A walk around a river, in a farm and a local village on a calm night is fascinating. A full moon day might not be perfect to see the animals, but every night has its own beautiful characteristics. Listening to thousands of musical creatures in a lonely village, watching fireflies and stars all over the sky, is a must-have experience during your visit to Sri Lanka.
A hut constructed in a tree where subsistence farmers spend the night chasing away wild animals that come to their crops. Spending a whole night on a tree hut is a different experience. If you get to chase away wild elephants eating crops, that is not just a lifetime experience but a tremendous help for a local family. Have a tour around the farm and pick any edibles you can find. A local family will cook you authentic food.
Life in the remote villages of Sri Lanka remains largely unchanged. You can feel how people live simply in harmony with the environment, responding to the seasons as they unfold. Visit a traditional village and experience the villagers' day-to-day life.
The Waulpane Cave is famous for its beautiful stalactites and stalagmites as well as the hundreds of thousands of bats that live there. Once entering the cave, you descend approximately 300 metres underground to discover this prehistoric world of ancient fossils, a stream running through the cave, and a beautiful waterfall in the centre. Visit a traditional cinnamon-making home and enjoy lunch in a real cinnamon garden.
Traditional ceremonial dancing night in a paddy field: The traditional dance forms are based on Sri Lankan folklore and associated with various rituals and ceremonies spanning over thousands of years. All these rituals are connected to the agricultural civilization of locals. All forms of dance in Sri Lanka exhibit lithe graceful movements combined with vigorous movement, reflecting the complex rhythms of the local drumbeat.
Spend an evening at a local fishing village. Fishermen in temporary fishing villages spend several months away from their families. They welcome outsiders and share their experience. If you are lucky they will take you on the boat for a tour.
Several beaches along Sri Lanka's coastline are nesting areas for endangered sea turtles. Spend a night with a non-profit organisation monitoring sea turtles laying eggs on the beach, and help conserve these remarkable aquatic reptiles. A super fun educational tour.
Join with a local family; go for a tour around the rural village. Canoe in a lake and pick lotus flowers. Prepare for a visit to an ancient Buddhist temple. Spend a spiritual and cultural evening.
Yala National Park is the iconic safari land in Sri Lanka, famous for leopards among both locals and foreigners. You will see elephants, sloth bears, crocodiles, monitor lizards, deer, wild boar, monkeys, and many other smaller creatures like porcupine and mongoose. It also boasts a large number of important cultural ruins, bearing testimony to earlier civilisations.
