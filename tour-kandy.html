Sacred City of Kandy

Home›Tours›Sacred City of Kandy

Kandy is the home of The Temple of the Tooth Relic (Sri Dalada Maligawa), one of the most sacred places of worship in the Buddhist world, and now a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Kandyan home gardens, spices, arts, culture and surrounding tea estates are the main tourist attractions.

The city is not only rich with higher cultural value — its richness is enhanced by higher scenic beauty. Surrounded by a few mountain peaks, it was declared a world heritage site by UNESCO in 1988.

Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic is a Buddhist temple in the city of Kandy. It is located in the royal palace complex of the former Kingdom of Kandy, which houses the relic of the tooth of the Buddha. Since ancient times, the relic has played an important role in local politics because it is believed that whoever holds the relic holds the governance of the country. The National Museum of Kandy features royal regalia and reminders of pre-European Sinhalese life, with over 5,000 artefacts from the Kandyan era.
The Kandy Esala Perahera is a grand festival celebrated with majestic elephants and costumes, held in July and August. This historical procession is held annually to pay homage to the Sacred Tooth Relic of Lord Buddha. It consists of many traditional local dances such as fire-dances, whip-dances, Kandyan dances and various other cultural dances, in addition to elephants adorned with lavish garments. Participate in a dinner with a local family.
The Royal Botanic Gardens is the largest botanical garden in Sri Lanka, featuring more than 4,000 species of plants and the National Herbarium. It attracts many local and foreign visitors throughout the year with its most diverse plant collection in the country.
Kandy has been a unique area due to its local culture and location among the hills. Many sites feature Kandyan Architecture and arts, especially at the Dalada Maligawa, Lankatilaka Temple, and Embekke Temple. Kandyan Dance is today the most popular local dance form of Sri Lanka. Different villages in Kandy specialise in specific artistic skills handed down through generations.
During early historical times, the island was world-renowned for its quality spices. Walk through the traditional Kandyan home garden to enjoy the smell of spices in the Kandyan hills. Have a luncheon and enjoy local spices — authentic Kandyan tastes that are different from the rest of the country.
Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage is an orphanage, nursery and captive breeding ground for wild Asian elephants, established in 1975. You can see elder and baby elephants taken to the river to bathe, and give a shower to them in a nearby river.
Misty montane forest in Sri Lanka is home to hundreds of animal and plant species with unique characters. These forests are mainly catchment areas of rivers. Montane forests are a great landscape to walk around and observe the lowlands of the country.
This is one of the best ways to explore tea country in Sri Lanka — a fun and relaxing tour. The dramatic landscape of the highlands stirs the senses as you wind past lush tea plantations, magnificent mountain peaks, and rushing waterfalls, travelling on the historic railroad.
Cycling through the winding roads of tea estates is a great experience for tourists and bicycle enthusiasts. Ride through a tea estate, walk into a tea factory while enjoying the marvellous sceneries. Enjoy factory-fresh real tea and spend a fabulous morning in a great location.
Horton Plains is undoubtedly one of the world's best natural landscapes. This land is recognised by scientists where ancient agriculture was carried out more than 17,000 years ago. Famous for the dramatic World's End escarpment and stunning cloud forest.
