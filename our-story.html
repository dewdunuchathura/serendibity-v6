Visit-Sri Lanka SerendiBity Vacations and Travel Ideas is a small local business startup initiated by a local young personnel who loves wildlife and adventure. Our vision is to be part of The Sri Lanka Tourism Development Authority (SLTDA) to achieve its goal to develop diverse, unique and quality tourism services and products that would make Sri Lanka as a unique destination, globally.

As a company, we demand the environmentally and socially just sustainable tourism practice to conserve nature, pave the pathway for Sri Lanka's sustainable development, without sacrificing the ultimate pleasure of the guest. We encourage our guests to be eco-friendly and socially responsible during their stay.

SerendiBity has successfully developed a number of responsible tourism practices with standards set well above the environmental protection requirements of local regulations through a variety of energy conservation, food storage and waste management techniques during our trips. We also promote conservation education among kids in rural areas of the island through a conservation education program.

We promote eco-tourism and low carbon practices for our clients. We encourage our clients to be plastic and polythene-free. We are putting our maximum effort to be a zero-waste company. We plant trees in our own campsite land to offset our emissions and to go carbon neutral.

We purchase locally grown food to support the local rural communities and farmers. Buying local food provides a tasty, fresh and nutritious meal to our valued guests. Especially, local foods promote food safety, conserve energy and protect the environment.

We support subsistence farmers and the local economy by encouraging our guests to enjoy authentic Sri Lankan cuisine as much as possible during their stay.

What is SerendiBity?

The word Serendib is the ancient Arabic and Persian name for Sri Lanka — used for centuries by traders who marvelled at this island's riches. The English word serendipity was coined in 1754 by Horace Walpole, inspired by the Persian fairy tale "The Three Princes of Serendip." We are SerendiBity — the serendipity of Serendib, with a capital B for the island itself.
