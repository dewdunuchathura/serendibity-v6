Sri Lanka is an independent island located just north of the equator in the Indian Ocean — a jewel of biodiversity, ancient history, and warm hospitality that has captivated travellers for millennia.

Key Facts

  • Official capital: Sri Jayawardenepura Kotte
  • Largest city: Colombo
  • Languages: Sinhala (74%), Tamil (18%), other (8%)
  • English spoken as second language: 10%
  • Topography: Flat coastal plain with central mountain range

Climate & Best Time to Visit

Sri Lanka's position creates two distinct monsoon seasons, meaning some part of the island is always sunny and beautiful.

  • Southwest: Monsoon May–September | Dry season December–March
  • Northeast: Monsoon October–January | Dry Season May–September
  • Lowland temperature: 23–35°C | 73–95°F
  • Highland temperature: 14–24°C | 57–75°F

Qualified doctors and both government and private hospitals are available all around the country. Mostly you are about half an hour away from a major hospital during your stay at the hotels. Many of Sri Lanka's physicians have trained in the West and are particularly experienced in dealing with locally occurring diseases. In the field, we will get you help as quickly as possible. Your Field Engagement Specialists have undergone first-aid training and will always help you find appropriate medical care.
Watch out for bowel diseases such as diarrhoea and amoebic dysentery and fungal infections if you are travelling from North American or European countries. Always check with your healthcare provider before you travel to Sri Lanka about vaccinations or other preventative treatments you may need. Please refer to your tour manager for more information.
Avoid being bitten by bugs/mosquitoes by covering up and using bug/mosquito repellents. All hotels have precautionary methods to avoid bugs/mosquito bites. Sri Lanka has eradicated Malaria and the risk is very low throughout the year. Read more at WHO.
We advise drinking bottled water. Always seek local advice and the consent of your Field Engagement Specialist before entering any type of water body and be alert to the dangers.
Buses and trains are particularly the main modes of transport in Sri Lanka. Always get the consent from your Field Engagement Specialist or the tour manager before taking public transport. Consider taking taxis or three wheelers/tuk-tuk as they're cheap in tourist areas.
Strict forestry and wildlife laws, rules and regulations apply. Collection or export of parts of flora, animals, birds or reptiles — such as skins, horns, scales, and feathers — is prohibited. Collection, purchase, and export without licence of any flora, wild animal, bird or reptile, dead or alive, is prohibited.
Please be respectful when visiting religious shrines or temples, and when taking pictures of religious statues. While Sri Lanka is a photographer's delight, permits are required at certain sites. Government law does not allow people to take photographs near government or military establishments.
Smoking in enclosed public areas and on streets has been prohibited by the government. Sometimes you will see foreigners smoking on the streets but it is considered disrespectful. Penalties for possession, dealing and trafficking drugs are severe and include the death penalty.
Please dress modestly when visiting religious shrines or temples. Please be sensitive to local cultural norms of dress and sensibilities. Nude or topless sunbathing is considered disreputable in public places.

Time to Travel

Inter-monsoon rains occur in October and November. Sri Lanka's unique position means year-round travel is possible if you choose the right coast.
